  • Conlanging - Conlanging is the process of creating fictional languages as a hobby. This formed after I got to make my own cipher in a highschool cybersecurity course, and I just kept on experimenting with trying to make a language. Some time ago I came across this video about a cursed Polynesian/Mandarin inspired conlang, which jumpstarted my language creation by opening my eyes to what I could really do. My most defined language right now is a language called Ictuk, a Navajo/Tigrinya inspired language for a species of humanoid warrior wasps. I also just started on a language called Qvefozian, a Slavic inspired language with heavy emphasis on semiotics, or sign language, as a form of inflection.

It had been a while since I went rollerblading, but it came pretty naturally to me the first time I did it around 5th or 6th grade. So, I went to Dick's Sporting Goods with my grandparents, and as a gift they bought me a new rollerblades, because my old ones were practically degrading. Words could not convey the amount of fun I had the first time I tried them on campus, and with that fun I started using them nearly every day, to the point where I started using them conventionally to get to class. I quickly became "The Rollerblading Guy", as many new interractions I've had typically start with "Hey, you're that guy on rollerblades!", or something of that sort.
